Aggressive sizing language (“25–40% initial tranche”) may be too forgiving of that noise.

Conservative Analyst: where it may be overly cautious

This is correct, but I’d caution against over-reacting to the stop-distance math. ATR-based thinking is useful for sizing, but it shouldn’t automatically imply “reduce conviction to the point of under-participation.” If your thesis is trend-continuation with bullish regime indicators (price > 10/50/200 and MACD histogram positive), then an overly tiny initial tranche can turn a good setup into a “watch it from the sidelines” trade.

3) “Missing cash-flow data increases uncertainty”
This is a legitimate limitation, but it’s not a reason to refuse the trade; it’s a reason to control exposure and avoid binary thinking. Your current staged approach already acknowledges execution/cash-flow uncertainty. So the conservative side should push mostly on sizing/conditions, not on abandoning the trade direction.

So what’s the moderate, sustainable strategy that best blends both?
I agree with “Buy” directionally (because the technical regime is bullish and not euphoric: RSI ~60, MACD positive with positive histogram, price above 50 and 200). Where I differ is how aggressively you should size the initial tranche and how you should structure adds to avoid momentum-decay whipsaw.

1) Initial buy: bias toward the lower end, not the middle-upper end
Given stop distance is ~1 ATR, I would move from “25–40%” to something like “20–30% now.” The goal is not to be timid—it’s to ensure that a normal volatility pullback doesn’t force emotional re-risking. This directly addresses the Conservative Analyst’s main valid point (whipsaw risk) without eliminating participation (Aggressive Analyst’s main valid point: trend/momentum edge).

The stop should remain tied to the thesis invalidation (50-SMA zone). Widening would reduce stop-outs but would also increase damage if the thesis is wrong. In this case, the real fix is sizing and add rules, not loosening invalidation.

3) Support-add near ~3925: require “support + momentum not deteriorating,” but keep it practical
Instead of adding purely because price tags ~3925, require:
- price holds above 50-SMA into at least the next session (or a clear intraday rejection), AND
- RSI stays above ~50 OR MACD histogram remains positive (even if shrinking, as long as it doesn’t flip/decay sharply).

This is the best compromise: it incorporates the Conservative Analyst’s “don’t add during deterioration” concern, but it still allows the staged re-entry behavior that the Aggressive Analyst is trying to capture.

You already have the right rule: “daily close above ~4315 with MACD histogram not decaying.” I’d translate “not decaying” into something measurable and immediate: histogram should remain positive and not show a clear roll-over between the breakout day and the next confirming close. That prevents breakout-false-starts.

Yes, upper band is ~4314, and upside could test it if momentum persists. But Bollinger bands are also volatility-dependent and not a guarantee. Treat it as a “first logical zone to manage adds/exits,” not as a promise.
